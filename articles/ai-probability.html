AI 真的可以這樣用嗎？用數學邏輯判斷 AI 的適用邊界 Can AI Really Do That? A Logic-Based Framework for Knowing When to Trust It

Venlia Chang
2025
4 分鐘閱讀

曾看到一位專業人士發文感嘆：現在這個世道，有人不一定相信專家，卻相信 AI 說的話。

這個現象讓我想了想——或許我可以寫些什麼，幫助大家輕鬆理解 AI 背後的運作邏輯，從根本上判斷它能做什麼、不能做什麼。

傳統程式 vs. AI：一個關鍵差異

傳統程式的核心是條件加邏輯：

若消費總價 > 2000，則 折扣 = 10%
若消費總額 < 1000，則 折扣 = 0%
其他狀況，則 折扣 = 5%

條件有限、固定，且能被明確寫出來。

但有些問題，條件是無限的、會隨時間移動的：

  • 如何用程式判別 X 光片是否有腫瘤？（癌症形態千變萬化，條件根本寫不完）
  • 如何用程式做開放式語言問答？（表達方式無窮無盡，條件無從設定）

於是有人換了一個思路：既然無法一刀切，那就用「統計」來估算，哪個答案的「機率」最高？ 這正是 AI 誕生的核心邏輯。

AI 的本質：機率與統計

大語言模型（LLM）：在特定語境下，下一個詞出現的機率最高是哪個？

醫療影像辨識（ML）：這張 X 光片，有腫瘤的機率是多少？

既然是機率與統計所構成，就存在本質上的不確定性——模型輸出的永遠是「最可能的答案」，而非「絕對正確的答案」。

哪些場景不適合交給 AI？

  • 零容錯的情境：財務報表、法規遵循文件
  • 語意高度相似的領域：法律文本（細微措辭差異牽一髮而動全身）
  • 完全沒有訓練資料的問題：AI 從未見過的領域，它只能「猜」

反之，創意發想、大數據趨勢預測、圖像辨識分類，則是 AI 能發揮高價值的場域。

用 AI 前，先問自己三個問題

  • 這個場景，我能接受一定程度的錯誤嗎？
  • 這個問題，答案之間的語意邊界夠清晰嗎？
  • 這個問題，AI 在訓練過程中真的見過類似的案例嗎？

能清楚回答這三個問題，就能做出更明智的 AI 使用判斷。🤗

A professional once posted something that stuck with me: people today are more willing to trust AI than a domain expert.

That observation prompted me to write this. Not to critique the trend, but to share the underlying logic — so anyone can make a grounded judgment about when AI is the right tool, and when it isn't.

Traditional code vs. AI: one fundamental difference

Traditional software is built on conditions and logic:

IF total_spend > 2000 THEN discount = 10%
IF total_spend < 1000 THEN discount = 0%
ELSE discount = 5%

The conditions are finite, fixed, and explicitly writable.

But some problems have conditions that are infinite or shift over time:

  • How do you write rules to detect cancer in an X-ray? (Tumor morphology varies infinitely — you can't enumerate the conditions.)
  • How do you write rules for open-ended language responses? (Human expression has no fixed structure.)

Someone had a different idea: if we can't write explicit rules, can we use statistics to find the most probable answer? That insight is the foundation of modern AI.

The nature of AI: probability and statistics

Large language models: Given this context, what token has the highest probability of coming next?

Medical image classification: Given this scan, what is the probability that a tumor is present?

Since AI is built on probability and statistics, it carries inherent uncertainty. It always produces the most likely answer — not the objectively correct one.

Where AI falls short

  • Zero-error-tolerance contexts: financial statements, legal compliance documents
  • High semantic similarity domains: legal text, where minor wording differences carry enormous consequence
  • Problems with no training data: AI can only extrapolate from what it has seen before

Conversely, creative ideation, trend forecasting from large datasets, and image classification are domains where AI can deliver genuine, high-leverage value.

Three questions before you use AI

  • Can I tolerate a margin of error in this context?
  • Are the semantic boundaries in this problem clear enough?
  • Has AI actually been trained on problems similar to this one?

Answer these honestly, and you'll make far better decisions about where AI earns its place. 🤗
