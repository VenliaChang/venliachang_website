我對 AI 與數位轉型產業趨勢的觀察 Reflections on the AI & Digital Transformation Wave

Venlia Chang
2025
3 分鐘閱讀

最近腦袋過載，暫時擱下數學與技術，說說我對這波 AI 浪潮的產業觀察。

每一波革命，都有人選擇不相信

百年前，工業革命席捲而來。許多工匠堅信自己的技藝無可取代——但那個時代真正留下來的，又有多少？

幾十年前，電腦逐漸普及。DOS 介面對一般人而言艱澀難懂，也有人認為電腦不可信任而拒絕採用。如今回頭看，那些抗拒者的命運，不難想像。

現在，AI 成了最熱的話題。有人說泡沫即將破裂，有人說不跟上就會被淘汰，有人說 AI 生成的程式碼讓人不放心。這些聲音，其實都似曾相識。

判斷產業前景，回到一個問題

需求是否強勁，能否撐得起那樣的市場規模？

只要需求夠強，市場就會持續推動產業成長。

若需求強勁，但某些公司仍難以獲利——那是經營能力的問題，不是產業泡沫的訊號。每個產業都有經營不善的公司；它們若無法找到可持續的商業模式，自然會有更有效率的競爭者接手填補這個空缺。

AI 的需求，到底強勁還是疲弱？

以往花三個月開發的系統，AI 輔助下三小時可達相近品質；以往難以預測的指標，AI 能精準建模；以往耗費大量人力的分類作業，AI 輕鬆處理。

許多原本做不到的事，現在做得到了。你認為這樣的需求，是強勁還是疲弱？

我們正身處一場新的產業革命。面對它，最好的策略從來不是觀望——而是參與其中。

Setting aside the math and technical details for a moment — here are my observations on where the AI and digital transformation wave is actually heading.

Every revolution has its skeptics

A century ago, the Industrial Revolution reshaped the global economy. Many craftsmen were convinced their skills were irreplaceable by cold machinery. Few of them were right.

Decades later, personal computing went mainstream. Early DOS interfaces were intimidating, and a significant number of professionals refused to adopt computers on principle. In hindsight, their fate is not hard to imagine.

Today, AI dominates the conversation. Some warn of an imminent bubble. Others say those who don't adapt will be left behind. Some express discomfort with AI-generated code. These reactions, too, follow a familiar pattern.

To evaluate any industry's trajectory, return to one question

Is the demand strong enough to sustain a market at this scale?

Where demand is genuine and durable, markets grow — regardless of short-term noise.

When companies struggle to monetize despite strong underlying demand, that's a business model problem, not an industry signal. Every sector has poorly run companies. If they can't find a sustainable path, more capable players will step in and capture what they couldn't.

Is AI demand strong or weak?

Development cycles that once took three months now take three hours. Metrics that were previously unpredictable can now be modeled with precision. Classification tasks that required teams can now be handled automatically.

Capabilities that didn't exist a few years ago are now table stakes. You tell me: does that sound like strong demand, or weak?

We are in the middle of a generational shift. The most effective response has never been to wait and watch — it's to engage, build, and lead.
